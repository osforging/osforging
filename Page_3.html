When you are starting out there aren't a whole lot of facts and things you need to know. I watched Youtube videos and experimented on my own to learn how to forge things. This can be a good way but it generally takes a while, learning from mistakes, and sometimes you develop bad habits about things like the physical posture of forging, but as long as one isn't hurting themselves it isn't a huge deal (I'm sure my stance is atrocious but that is what I get for not learning form a professional). So anyway, your best bet is to learn from a professional blacksmith in your area. They can be hard to find, but if you can go up and ask if they could teach you or at least give you some pointers!

Once you have everything gathered together, I would start with just simply pounding on the metal with no expectations to see what it does and how it feels. Then I would start making simple things like a leaf. Make several leaves and then move on to making something like hooks. Practicing the simple things over and over will improve your basic skills and you can try progressively more and more complicated projects.

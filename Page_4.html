Mistakes: I have made a lot of them and I will summarize the "best" ones here for your benefit.

  • Trying to do things cheaply: This is a common problem for me. For example, I wanted to make my own forge burners to save money. I ended up spending about $80-$90 on supplies. I then spent a few months trying to get them to work. They would work for a few minutes and then quit. I ended up just getting actual machined forge burners that, while they were double the price, worked instantly and still work. Quality is really important for 2 things that you buy as a blacksmith. #1 is the anvil. #2 is the forge burners. Beyond that if you want to buy cheap things go ahead. As long as it isnt a critical part, it doesn't matter too much.
  • Always wear PPE: (personal protective equipment) This is a must do for anything you do. when you are grinding, wear earmuffs, safety glasses, and a resirator that is rated for filtering particulates. I often don't wear gloves when I forge or weld and then I inevitably end up burning myself. The most important things to wear are safety goggles and a respirator. Metal shavings are just plain horrible for your lungs and if they get in your eyes, well, let's not even go there. Just wear PPE. you won't regret it.
  • Don't spend forver looking at online reviews: people online don't know anything. I don't care if it is a blacksmith forum or Amazon; people online aren't all-knowing. When you are buying something, pay attention to reviews. Just don't spend forver looking at every review and tiny detail. For example: What type of castable refractory to use or whether or not to use kao wool. This is just one of many over-debated questions on forums online. Do some research, but don't get lost in the reviews. Gather what you have learned and make the best choice you can.
